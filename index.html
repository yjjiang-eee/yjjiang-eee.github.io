Short Bio

Dr. Yajie Jiang (蒋亚杰) is an Associate Researcher (BR-B Talent) at the Institute of Electrical Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences (IEE-CAS), Beijing, China. He received the B.Eng. degree in Electrical Engineering from Zhengzhou University in 2015, the M.Eng. degree from Huazhong University of Science and Technology in 2018, and the Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering from The University of Hong Kong in 2022 (full scholarship). From 2022 to 2023, he was a Distinguished Postdoctoral Fellow at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University. From 2023 to 2025, he was a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. His research focuses on intelligent electric drives, networked DC/AC microgrids, lithium-ion batteries and solid-state batteries, and AI-empowered modeling, control and state monitoring for power electronics and motor drives.

Dr. Jiang has published more than 20 first-/corresponding-author SCI journal papers, including IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics, IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics, IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid, and IEEE Transactions on Industry Applications. One paper has been selected as an ESI Highly Cited Paper. He is a member of IEEE, Senior Member of the China Electrotechnical Society, and serves as Deputy Secretary-General of the Technical Committee on Artificial Intelligence and Electrical Applications. He is also Guest Editor of several SCI journals and active reviewer for many IEEE Transactions and international conferences.

Research Interests / 研究方向

  • Electric vehicle permanent-magnet motor drive technology and high-performance control.
  • Ultra-high-speed linear motion and electromagnetic propulsion systems (Mach-level LIM/LSM).
  • AI-empowered advanced control and condition monitoring for motors and power electronic converters.
  • Modeling and control of AC/DC microgrids and grid-forming converters.
  • Electro-thermal-mechanical modeling and testing of large-capacity lithium-ion and solid-state batteries.
  • AI-based battery lifetime prediction, safety assessment, and temperature balancing control.
  • Battery packs thermal management, temperature equalization and fast charging converters.

Selected Research Projects

  • PI – IEE-CAS BR-B Talent Project
    AI-assisted disturbance-rejection control for Mach-level double-sided linear induction motor propulsion systems; including lightweight motor design, AI-aided high-speed drive control, and experimental verification platform construction.
  • PI – Distinguished Postdoctoral Fellowship, PolyU Hong Kong
    Carbon concern-based optimal operation of DC fast charging stations using Markov game modeling and multi-agent deep reinforcement learning.
  • Key Researcher – CAS Pioneer Project (B-class)
    High-current ultra-high-speed electromagnetic power system design and control; LIM design for 2.5 Mach (~850 m/s) operation and high-acceleration current control strategies.
  • Core Member – CAS IEE BR-A Project
    Mach-level high-thrust linear induction motor systems for high-speed transportation with AI-assisted control and HIL platforms.
  • Co-PI – Singapore MOE Tier-1 Project
    Next-generation BMS based on electrochemical–thermo–mechanical–aging models for all-solid-state batteries.
  • Core Member – Imperial–NTU Collaboration Fund
    Extending lifespan of second-life traction batteries using next-generation electric springs for smart grids.
